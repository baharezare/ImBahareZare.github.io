Project 4: Customer Marketing Analysis

Overview

This project involves analyzing a dataset of 2,240 customers with 28 variables related to marketing. The analysis focuses on understanding customer behavior, campaign effectiveness, and channel performance to drive strategic marketing decisions.

Dataset Description

The dataset includes customer profiles, purchase history, campaign responses, and channel engagement metrics. Key variables include:

  • ID: Unique customer identifier
  • Year_Birth: Customer's birth year
  • Education: Education level of the customer
  • Marital_Status: Marital status of the customer
  • Income: Yearly household income
  • Kidhome: Number of children in household
  • Teenhome: Number of teenagers in household
  • Dt_Customer: Date of enrollment with the company
  • Recency: Days since the last purchase
  • MntWines: Amount spent on wine in the last 2 years
  • ...and more variables related to campaign responses, complaints, and country.

Key Insights

After data analysis, we found the following correlations:

  • Income positively correlates with the number and amount of purchases, suggesting high-income customers buy more frequently and spend more.
  • Amount Spent on Wines and Meat Products correlates highly with catalog purchases, indicating these products are primarily purchased via catalog.
  • NumWebVisitsMonth has little correlation with web purchases, but a mild correlation with deal purchases, showing deals attract customers to the website.

Data Preparation and Analysis

Data cleaning was performed to handle missing values and outliers. A detailed examination revealed missing data in one attribute and artificially created missing values in two others, up to 10%. All missing values and outliers were removed in the data-cleaning process. Descriptive statistics and correlation analysis were conducted, followed by exploratory plots to visualize distributions and patterns.

Results

The analysis highlights key trends and customer segments that support targeted marketing strategies. By understanding the preferences and behaviors of different customer segments, we can enhance campaign targeting and optimize channel usage.
